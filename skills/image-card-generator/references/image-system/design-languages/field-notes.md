# Field Notes

## Use When

Use for observations, compact takeaways, lessons, and practical notes that should feel collected in the field.

## Visual DNA

Paper, torn edges, tabs, pencil marks, specimen circles, muted ink, human but controlled imperfection.

## Must Use

- A real note-taking artifact.
- Visible ordering or grouping.
- Warm paper texture.

## Must Not Use

- Dense handwriting.
- Scrapbook clutter.
- Cute stickers unless requested.

## Prompt Skeleton

"Create a finished field-notes style information card with a clear title, 2-4 concise note items, tactile paper, specimen marks, tabs, pencil-like ruled lines, and controlled human imperfection. Keep labels large and readable. Avoid dense handwriting or scrapbook clutter."

## Layout Pairings

Best with `artifact-board`, `receipt-comparison`, and `flow-diagram`.

## Text Strategy

Checklist-style facts. Use a short title and 2-4 concise note items. Use abstract note marks only for background texture, not as a substitute for information.

## QA Checks

- Notes feel intentional, not messy.
- Required note items are readable; no unreadable handwriting pretending to be content.
- The card still feels premium enough for sharing.

## Repair Prompts

- "Clean up the notebook clutter and keep only three ordered note artifacts."
- "Replace handwriting with blank ruled lines and large simple labels."
- "Make the note system more structured and less casual."
