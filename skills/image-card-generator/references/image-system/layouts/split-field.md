# Split Field

## Use When

Use for A/B claims, before/after, Pro vs Lite, old vs new, cost vs capability, or two-step explanation.

## Information Contract

Must make both sides of the comparison explicit: a title, side A label, side B label, and one short contrast note or metric per side.

## Composition Contract

Two fields share the canvas but one side should lead. Use a clear dividing axis, overlap, diagonal split, or scale imbalance. The contrast must be visible without reading text.

## Prompt Blocks

- "Create a finished split-field social card with two contrasting visual zones."
- "Make the left and right fields materially different while preserving one coherent palette."
- "Use one side as the dominant anchor and the other as supporting evidence."

## Text Rules

Medium text capacity: one short title, two side labels, and up to two concise comparison facts. Avoid dense rows or exact tables.

## QA Checks

- The two sides are distinct and meaningful.
- The split does not become two equal generic cards.
- Labels, if present, are accurate and readable.

## Repair Prompts

- "Increase contrast between the two fields using different materials and scale, not more text."
- "Make the right side secondary and the left side dominant to avoid two equal panels."
- "Remove fake UI details and replace them with physical artifacts or abstract comparison marks."
