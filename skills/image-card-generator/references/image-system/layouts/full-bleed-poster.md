# Full-Bleed Poster

## Use When

Use for covers, closers, announcements, and one-line theses where instant visual impact matters more than detailed explanation.

## Information Contract

Must carry one readable headline and one supporting claim, date, number, or takeaway. The visual scene cannot be the only message.

## Composition Contract

One dominant subject fills the frame. Use crop, scale contrast, edge tension, or a single strong horizon. Avoid placing the subject and text in a centered default hero arrangement.

## Prompt Blocks

- "Create a finished full-bleed social poster with one dominant visual anchor occupying 45-70% of the frame."
- "Use strong edge crop and asymmetrical negative space for a short title."
- "Make the image readable at phone preview size."

## Text Rules

Title plus one short support line. Keep both large and high contrast. If the user needs body copy, choose another layout.

## QA Checks

- One visual anchor is obvious within one second.
- No generic atmospheric background is carrying the card alone.
- Title, if present, is large and readable.
- The crop feels intentional, not accidental.

## Repair Prompts

- "Strengthen the anchor: enlarge the main subject to dominate the frame and remove secondary distractions."
- "Reduce generic hero feel: move the subject off-center and use a stronger edge crop."
- "Simplify text: keep only one short headline in large clean typography."
