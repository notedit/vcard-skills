# Artifact Board

## Use When

Use when the card should feel like collected evidence: benchmark slips, model notes, receipts, diagrams, video frames, or research fragments.

## Information Contract

Must include a clear title and 2-3 evidence facts, labels, or numbers. The artifacts should organize information, not merely decorate the card.

## Composition Contract

One primary artifact should dominate, supported by 2-4 secondary artifacts. Use overlap, pins, tape, edges, or desk perspective. Do not let many equal papers flatten the hierarchy.

## Prompt Blocks

- "Create a finished social card as a tactile evidence board with one dominant paper slab and three supporting artifacts."
- "Use layered depth, pinned notes, cropped edges, and blank ruled marks instead of readable tiny writing."
- "Keep the board organized and editorial, not chaotic."

## Text Rules

Medium text capacity. Use one title plus 2-3 large evidence labels or numbers. Small handwritten marks should stay abstract unless exact wording is required.

## QA Checks

- Dominant artifact is clear.
- Secondary artifacts support, not compete.
- No fake labels, logos, or unreadable pseudo text.
- The board feels curated, not messy.

## Repair Prompts

- "Reduce clutter: simplify to one dominant paper slab and three secondary notes."
- "Remove fake text: replace all tiny writing with abstract ruled lines and blank tags."
- "Strengthen hierarchy: enlarge the main artifact to occupy 45% of the frame."
