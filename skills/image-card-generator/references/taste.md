# Taste Baseline

Visual quality floor for every generated image card. Read before planning or generating. Information and readability win over decoration.

## Core

Remove obvious AI-generated traces.

Primary rule:

- A normal image card must communicate a clear claim, number, comparison, step, or takeaway. Visual style cannot replace information.

Hard bans:

- No pure black `#000`, `#000000`, or `rgb(0,0,0)`.
- No default centered hero layout.
- No three equal cards, three equal columns, or three equal metric boxes as the main composition.
- No generic AI copy such as "赋能", "释放", "下一代", "智能化", "重塑", or "革新" unless quoted.
- No fake-looking data, fake rankings, fake quotes, fake product claims, or placeholder metrics.
- No neon/glow AI gradients, glassy SaaS panels, or meaningless decorative grids as the main style.
- No visual-only cards unless explicitly requested.
- No cover word pileup. The cover may contain only the user title and one guiding sentence.

## Density

Taste does not mean empty.

- Cover: density 2, only user title plus one guiding sentence.
- Poster, quote, cinematic, closer: density 2-4, earned through a strong title plus one clear claim.
- Explanation, mechanism, proof, process, comparison: density 5-6, with one claim plus 2-4 concrete points.
- Checklist, how-to, product notes, field notes: density 6-7, structured and scannable.
- Data cards: one dominant number plus 2-3 context notes.

Every explanatory set should include at least one useful medium-density card. Do not make the whole carousel a sequence of attractive headlines.

## Composition

Prefer:

- strong visual anchor.
- clear information hierarchy.
- large shapes or object dominance.
- crop, overlap, edge placement, diagonal tension.
- asymmetrical whitespace.
- artifact metaphors: ticket, receipt, label, stamp, field note, specimen, photo, package, tool, map.
- image as evidence, artifact, atmosphere, or finished poster; avoid background-only cards.

Use fine lines, grids, and boxes only as support. They cannot carry the design alone.

## Generated Image Discipline

- Ask the model to render concise, large, mobile-readable text as the primary content.
- Avoid dense text, brand logos, UI labels, watermarks, page numbers, or captions.
- Keep required text short, large, and explicitly inspect it after generation.
- Use synthetic objects and scenes honestly. Do not imply generated images are real photos.
- For people, products, brands, or copyrighted characters, avoid direct imitation unless the user owns or supplies the asset and the use is allowed.
- For reference images, record whether they are used for direct reference, style reference, or palette reference.

## Pre-Delivery QA

Check:

- The image has no unwanted text artifacts.
- The card's main claim is understandable.
- Required title/key facts are present and readable.
- The visual anchor is clear within 1 second.
- Any baked-in text is readable and accurate.
- The set has visual continuity without repeating the same layout.
- The cover has hook power and the middle cards carry real information.
- The cover title is the largest visual focus and contains no extra labels, captions, badges, or slogans.
- No clipped text, unwanted marks, fake logos, or external hotlinked assets.
